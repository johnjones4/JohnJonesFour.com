Before submitting to the app store, make sure you’ve completed these steps. Without them your app could have serious issues and might not receive the downloads it deserves.

\r\n\r\n
    \r\n\r\n
  • Prepare a variety of app store descriptions and then test them out for appeal and clarity on friends and family. Doing this will show you how real consumers will interpret your language in the wild.
    • \r\n\r\n
  • After you choose the most effective description for your target audience, thoroughly proof read it for spelling, grammar, and accuracy.
    • \r\n\r\n
  • Prepare your app store keywords using keywordtool.io to check them. Make sure to not use stop words and connectors that take up space and use words efficiency. (i.e. 9 instead of nine.)
    • \r\n\r\n
  • Have a privacy policy, especially if you use GPS, contacts, or any other data service in your app and especially if your app is targeted at children. Sites like Iubenda and host of other "free privacy policy generator" sites help make this a simple process.
    • \r\n\r\n
  • Setup a support-specific email address such as support@myappname.com so that users looking for assistance are not emailing your personal Gmail directly.
    • \r\n\r\n
  • Prepare your app landing page for launch. This means that you need to be ready to swap out any pre-release language to download-driving language. If you do not already have a landing page for your app setup, you’ll need that before submitting your app for distribution. Create a free landing page
    • \r\n\r\n
